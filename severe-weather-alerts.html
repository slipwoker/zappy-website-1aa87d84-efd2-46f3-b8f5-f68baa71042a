Emergency Readiness Checklist

Stay Informed

Monitor WYNC NEWS for real-time severe weather alerts and Doppler radar updates. Keep a battery-powered NOAA weather radio as backup.

Build an Emergency Kit

Assemble water (one gallon per person per day), non-perishable food, flashlight, batteries, first aid supplies, and medications for at least 72 hours.

Create a Family Communication Plan

Establish meeting points and out-of-area emergency contacts. Ensure all family members know school and workplace evacuation procedures.

Secure Your Home

Trim trees near structures, secure outdoor furniture, and know how to shut off utilities. Inspect sump pumps and clear gutters before storm seasons.

Prepare for Power Outages

Charge devices fully when severe weather is forecast. Keep portable chargers ready and consider a generator for medical equipment. Never use generators indoors.

Know Your Evacuation Routes

Identify multiple escape paths from your neighborhood. Keep your vehicle fueled and pack a "go bag" with essential documents, cash, and supplies.
